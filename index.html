Campaign Pitch — Case Study 03
Babbel — "Fluent in Real Life"

A conceptual TikTok content series pitched for Babbel — showing how a strategist thinks from audience insight to content execution to measurable KPIs. This is the kind of work I'd bring to any social media role on day one.

🗣️

Babbel — TikTok Campaign Pitch

Language learning app · 10M+ subscribers globally · Strong brand but underdeveloped TikTok presence · Audience: adult learners with real-world goals

5-part
Content series

Strategy type
The strategic insight

Babbel's core differentiator is that it teaches conversation, not curriculum — real phrases for real situations. But their TikTok presence doesn't reflect that. Most language app content is either classroom-style or meme-based. Neither shows the actual emotional experience of using a new language in the wild.

The opportunity: a 5-part series that closes that gap — documenting real moments of language use across travel, work, and daily life. Content that makes viewers think "I want that confidence." and immediately download the app.

The "Fluent in Real Life" series — 5 videos
🛬

Ep. 1 — "At the airport, no English"

POV: navigating a foreign airport using only the target language. High relatability, strong hook. Designed to go viral in the travel community.

Hook type: Relatable fear
🍜

Ep. 2 — "Ordering food in a language I'm learning"

Unscripted moment of ordering at a local restaurant — mistakes included. Authentic, funny, shareable. Babbel branding as the prep tool shown naturally.

Hook type: Vulnerability + humor
💼

Ep. 3 — "Using Spanish in a professional setting"

Career-focused angle — speaking a second language in a meeting or networking event. Targets the ambitious adult learner who uses Babbel for career growth, not just travel.

Hook type: Aspiration
📱

Ep. 4 — "30 days of Babbel — what actually changed"

A progress check-in video with measurable before/after moments. High save rate potential — viewers bookmark progress videos to revisit. Doubles as a soft testimonial without feeling like an ad.

Hook type: Transformation proof
🌍

Ep. 5 — "What nobody tells you about learning a language as an adult"

The most personal video in the series — honest reflection on the mental and emotional journey of adult language learning. Positions Babbel as the brand that actually understands its users.

Hook type: Emotional resonance
Why this series would work for Babbel
Each video targets a different viewer motivation — fear, humor, ambition, progress, identity — maximizing reach across audience segments
Babbel's product promise ("real conversations") is demonstrated, not stated — the strongest form of brand storytelling
Series format builds return viewers across 5 posts — compounding reach and follower growth with each drop
Real-life setting removes the "classroom content" stigma that kills most language app TikToks
Risks & how I'd mitigate them
Risk: Series drops too fast, loses momentum — Mitigation: space videos 3–4 days apart, tease each episode in comments of the previous
Risk: Over-branded feel kills authenticity — Mitigation: Babbel appears as a natural tool, never the subject; creator voice stays primary
Risk: Ep. 4 (progress video) underperforms if gains feel small — Mitigation: frame progress emotionally, not just fluency metrics
KPIs I'd track & why
KPI 01
Save rate (target: >3%)

Saves signal that viewers want to return to the content — the strongest indicator of genuine intent. A viewer who saves a language video is a highly qualified app download candidate.

KPI 02
Full watch-through rate (target: >15%)

Benchmarked against my own Taco Bell result of 15.3% vs. a 4–10% industry standard. If a viewer watches the full video, the message landed. TikTok also rewards this with broader distribution.

KPI 03
Share rate (target: >0.8%)

Shares are the highest-signal engagement metric — they mean a viewer trusted this content enough to put their name on it. My baseline share rate of 0.85% already exceeds typical benchmarks.

KPI 04
Profile visits & follower conversion

Track profile visit spikes during series window — a viewer who visits the profile after a Babbel video is one step from a brand follow and app awareness. Conversion funnel starts here.
